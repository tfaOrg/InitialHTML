Division law

Instead of using the multiplication and power laws, the division law can be used to expand or condense logarithms in a single step:

\(\log_k\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)= \log_k(a)-\log_k(b)\)

For example,

  • Expanding: \(\log_8\left(\frac{19}{13}\right)=\log_8(19)-\log_8(13)\)
  • Condensing: \(\log_7(5)-\log_7(11)=\log_7\left(\frac{5}{11}\right)\)

The logarithm of \(1\)

The following identity is true for logarithms:

\(\log_k(1)=0\)
